Cement Sector: The Clear Winners

The cement industry stands to gain disproportionately from this capex push. With infrastructure projects consuming 20-25% of India's cement production, the sector is poised for volume growth of 8-10% annually over the next three years.

Top Cement Picks

UltraTech Cement: As India's largest cement producer with 130 MTPA capacity, UltraTech is best positioned to capture infrastructure demand. Its pan-India presence aligns perfectly with geographically diversified project locations.

Ambuja Cements: The Adani Group-backed company has aggressive expansion plans, targeting 140 MTPA capacity by 2028. Its integration with Adani's infrastructure ventures provides unique synergies.

Shree Cement: Known for operational efficiency and the lowest cost structure in the industry, Shree Cement offers the best margin protection during input cost volatility.

Steel Sector: Nuanced Opportunity

Steel companies present a more complex picture. While infrastructure demand provides volume support, global pricing pressures and Chinese overcapacity create margin headwinds.

Steel Winners

JSW Steel: With the most modern facilities and highest value-added product mix, JSW Steel is insulated from commodity-grade pricing pressure. Its focus on construction-grade steel aligns with infrastructure demand.

Tata Steel (India operations): The domestic business benefits from infrastructure demand while the company restructures European operations. Improving EBITDA margins signal operational turnaround.

Proceed with Caution

SAIL: Despite being a pure domestic play, SAIL's aging plants and higher costs position it as a laggard. The company requires significant capex just to maintain competitiveness.

The Losers: Who Gets Left Behind?

Not all infrastructure-adjacent companies will benefit equally. Small cement players without regional dominance face margin pressure as large players leverage scale advantages.

Real estate developers focused on residential projects may see labor and material costs rise as infrastructure projects compete for resources. Construction cost inflation could squeeze developer margins.

Investment Strategy

For investors seeking infrastructure exposure, we recommend a barbell approach: overweight large-cap cement names for stability, and selectively add steel exposure through efficiency leaders.
